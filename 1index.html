The tool

Every student has a biliteracy story. Now you can see it.

The Biliteracy Compass is a dual-language, asset-based assessment tool built on one foundational belief: both languages matter, and both languages tell the story. Using English and Spanish performance data, the Compass orients each student within a biliteracy zone, identifies their dominant language from the data, and generates a precise instructional profile with research-grounded strategies and a concrete six-week action plan. No deficit framing. No sorting. No one-size-fits-all intervention. Just clarity, and a compass that always points forward.

What the Compass does

01

Sees both languages at once

English and Spanish percentiles plotted together. Dominant language calculated from data, not assumed.

02

Places students in a zone

Each student lands in one of four compass zones based on their biliteracy profile, not a deficit label.

03

Answers what's next

Every profile includes research-grounded strategies, grade-band specifics, and a six-week instructional action plan.

04

Shows the whole class

Upload a class roster and see every student on a biliteracy scatter plot. Click any dot to open their full profile.

The four compass zones

North

Spanish Dominant

Strong Spanish performance. English acceleration is the priority, using Spanish as the bridge.

East

Advanced Biliteracy

At or above the 50th percentile in both languages. Focus is academic rigor and metalinguistic depth.

South

English Dominant

Strong English performance. Spanish acceleration is the priority through authentic, culturally rooted instruction.

West

Developing Biliteracy

Below the 40th percentile in both languages. Content intervention in the dominant language is the priority.
