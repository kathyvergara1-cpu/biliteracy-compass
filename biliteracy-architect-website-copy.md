### [SECTION 1 — The Story / Who I Am]

**Section label (small caps):** The architect behind the work

**Headline:**
I know what it feels like to be unseen in a classroom.

**Body:**
I was eight years old when I started slipping out to the restroom before my ESL teacher arrived — anything to avoid the pull-out that made me feel less than my classmates. I loved learning. I loved language. What I didn't have was a system that loved me back.

That experience didn't break me. It built me.

Today, I'm a bilingual curriculum director, doctoral researcher, and published author who has spent two decades designing the systems that should have existed for students like me. I've built biliteracy curricula commissioned by the Texas Education Agency, presented alongside national experts, and been recognized by TABE for my commitment to linguistically diverse students across Texas.

But the work has never been about credentials. It's been about the eight-year-old who deserved better — and the millions of multilingual students who still do.

### [SECTION 3 — The Biliteracy Compass Feature]

**Section label:** The tool

**Headline:**
Every student has a biliteracy story. Now you can see it.

**Body:**
The Biliteracy Compass is a dual-language assessment tool built on one foundational belief: both languages matter, and both languages tell the story.

Using English and Spanish performance data, the Compass places each student in one of four zones — Advanced Biliteracy, Spanish Dominant, English Dominant, or Developing Biliteracy — and generates a precise instructional profile: what the student has, what they need, and exactly where to go next.

No deficit framing. No guesswork. No one-size-fits-all intervention.
Just clarity. And a compass that always points forward.

## ABOUT PAGE (short version)

**Headline:**
The architect. The advocate. The educator.

**Body:**
Dr. Kathy Vergara is a bilingual curriculum director, doctoral researcher, and published author with two decades of experience designing equitable biliteracy systems for multilingual learners in Texas and beyond.

She holds a doctorate in ethical leadership from the University of St. Thomas in Houston, a master's degree in educational psychology with emphasis on bilingual and dual-language education from Texas A&M, and a second master's degree in educational leadership for culturally and linguistically diverse students. Her undergraduate degree in Spanish and business management from the University of Houston sits at the intersection of who she has always been — a builder who speaks both languages, in every sense of the word.

Dr. Vergara has served at the state level on TEA's Dual Language Guidance Committee and Communities of Practice committee. She has been recognized by the Texas Education Agency and honored by the Texas Association for Bilingual Education. She is a contributing author in Solution Tree's *Culture Champions* anthology and a consultant with Solution Tree's Soluciones conference.

She is the creator of the Biliteracy Blueprint Framework, the Biliteracy Compass assessment tool, and the Biliterate by Design methodology — all of which she developed from her research, her classroom experience, and her own story as a first-generation Latina student who once hid from her ESL teacher at age eight.

That student is the reason for all of it.
